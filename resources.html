Hi! Thanks for stopping by to my page!

My name is Young Woong (Jonathan) Min and I'm currently a third-year at UC Berkeley studying Statistics and Applied Mathematics. I am passionate about data science, economics, mathematics, language learning, film/animation, and weight lifting. In the future, I hope to pursue a PhD in Statistics and eventually find an intersection between pop culture/entertainment and statistical research. I spend most of my time working on independent projects, classes, learning new subjects, listening to music, or eating out with friends.

My time in university has been a wild journey. Initially, I was set on pre-law, spending much of my time reading law review articles and history books. Around my sophomore year of university, I began to appreciate the importance of statistics as it seamlessly combines quantitative methods with qualitative reasoning. I pivoted hard into mathematics and statistics, building the quantitative skills necessary to be an effective statistician. This website is a product of this curiosity! I hope that the contents of this website prove useful (or at the very least are interesting). Once again, thanks for stopping by and stay in tune for more!
